Screening distances

Guidelines for evaluating the noise impact of a maintenance facility near a school may specify certain screening distances to select schools, but they are ‘NOT a substitute for case- and site-specific evaluation of potential risks and hazards’. These distances may not be a good predictor of an environmental hazard.

  • Federal Transportation Administration (FTA) guidelines mention

    • that ‘a maintenance facility near a school’ (p. 33) is one location recommended for a ‘Detailed Noise Analysis’
      • The ‘screening distance for noise assessments’ at a school is
        • 1,000 feet for unobstructed distances
        • 650 feet for obstructed distances (‘with intervening buildings’)

  • According to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) school siting guidelines

    • If Creekside Elementary were to be built in 2020 at its current location and the school district was evaluating its current location as a new school location, recommendations include
      • identification and evaluation of any rail yards (‘excluding electric light rail, except for safety’) within a 1-mile radius for new schools (p19, EPA school siting guidelines).
    • School siting guidelines do not specify radius for electric light rail, but many of the same potential hazards apply here: noise, ground water contamination, accidental releases/spills of hazardous chemicals, large truck traffic.

  • Have there been other light rail systems that have assessed elementary schools more than 1,000 feet away with intervening buildings when doing noise assessments for a railyard operations maintenance facility (ROMF)?

    • Sound Transit in Seattle, WA conducted a 2016 noise study at an elementary school, one mile away from the Sounder Yard and Shops Facility Project.
