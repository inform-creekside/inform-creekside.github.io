Public Health

Children spend a large part of their day at school. Understanding any increases in the noise footprint at Creekside Elementary school caused by the railyard operation maintenance facility (ROMF) is important. A growing body of evidence points towards an association between chronic environmental noise exposure (including noise from rail) and declines in a child’s cognitive performance. To achive this end the noise is not an exposure causing hearing loss, but lower levels

Many studies have demonstrated the negative impact of environmental noise exposure on children. Specifically, “…children with chronic aircraft, road traffic, or rail noise exposure at school have poorer reading ability, memory, and performance on national standardised tests than do children who are not exposed to noise at school.” Basner et al 2014 Another study demonstrated that the closer a child’s school to an environmental noise source such as an airport, the larger a reading delay.

Bottom line? Any noise footprint larger than what already exists for children at Creekside Elementary poses a public health problem.
