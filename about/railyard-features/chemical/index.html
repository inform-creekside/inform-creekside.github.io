Chemical Storage

’…regulated materials would be generated from maintenance activities at the ROMF.’

Source: Federal Environmental Impact Statement (FEIS)

  • Upon a request from a homeowner in November 2018, GoTriangle provided a list of 144 chemicals that may be stored and transported to and from the railyard maintenance and operations facility.
    • Volumes range from household size to 1,000 gallon oil tanks.
    • Many of these chemicals are hazardous, inflammable, and/or potentially explosive petroleum products. (Source: GoTriangle. ‘ROMF Fluids & Miscellaneous Materials Table.’ November 12, 2018).
    • Thus far, there are no specific emergency response plans or evacuation area estimates in the event of an explosion, spill or ground contamination.

  • The Federal Environmental Impact Statement (FEIS) noted that there will be materials that could contaminate the groundwater at the ROMF.

    • ‘While the light rail vehicles, as noted in section 4.8.3.1, do not operate on gasoline or oils that could spill and contaminate the groundwater through the operation of the light rail, as noted above, regulated materials would be generated from maintenance activities at the ROMF. As such, all regulated materials, including fluids (e.g., oils, greases, solvents and other waste materials), used at the ROMF will be captured and stored in tanks (inside buildings), where they will be periodically collected by an outside vendor for off-site recycling or disposal. All regulated materials will be disposed of in accordance with state and local guidelines and no substantial indirect impacts are anticipated.’
    • The potential to contaminate groundwater – a ‘spill event’ – at this site is unclear.
