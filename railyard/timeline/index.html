RAILYARD

Timeline

At no point in this this timeline was there ever any effort to directly contact parents, guardians or teaching staff at Creekside Elementary.

A text version of the timeline:

Year Action Summary
2010 Site identification Go Triangle Alternatives Analysis identified 5 sites for the ROMF
2012 May 2012 public comment period After four meetings, GoTriangle identified fifth Alston Ave site
2012 Environmental Analysis Process GoTriangle hosted more than 300 meetings with public groups
2015 GoTriangle notified Durham Public School District GoTriangle notified Durham Public School District about proposed ROMF at Farrington Road site. GoTriangle met with Creekside’s principal, the Deputy Superintendent of Operational Services; the Director of Transportation; and Director of Construction & Capital Planning
2015 FTA published environmental study Federal Transit Administration published the environmental study
2015 Hearings for ‘Final Environmental Impact Statement’ Triangle Transit holds public hearings on environmental study
2015 GoTriangle meeting at Creekside GoTriangle held a workshop at Creekside Elemnentary about the ‘maintenance facility’
2016 Combined Final Environmental Impact Statement Record of Decision Added clarification in the Farrington Road ROMF section “Direct impacts to Creekside Elementary School are not anticipated due to the distance from the Farrington Road ROMF and existing vegetation.”
2016 Farrington Road site supported by Durham government Durham County and City issued a “Record of Decision” that included Farrington Road as the ROMF
2017 GoTriangle had neighborhood meetings prior to Zoning change GoTriangle sent letters to property owners within 1,000 feet of the site.
2018 GoTriangle held workshop at Creekside Elementary Main purpose of workshops at Creekside Elementary was to discuss “types of screening and fences along Farrington Road”
2018 Durham City Zoning Hearing Durham City Planning commission did NOT vote in favor of industrial zoning change for ROMF.
2018 Charlotte ROMF tour At a tour of the Charlotte ROMF facility by GoTriangle, Durham City Council members, and Durham City residents, the facility manager stated that the Farrington Road ROMF would “not work”.
2018 Durham City Council Meeting Durham City Council voted in favor of zoning change for rail yard site.
